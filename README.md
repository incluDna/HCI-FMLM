## Designing and Evaluating a Multimodal Transit Interface for First- and Last-Mile Decision-Making in Bangkok  [[Full paper]](https://github.com/incluDna/HCI-FMLM/blob/6cd9bccb9aed1cbc13f2b27ea80bbc1ebfb062dd/group13findbus_finaldraft.pdf)

> Bangkok commuters often rely on multiple first- and last-mile connectors before reaching main transit, yet conventional route-planning 
interfaces usually present these segments as bundled route alternatives. This study evaluates whether explicitly visualizing and allowing 
selection of first-mile, main-transit, and last-mile components improves route-planning decisions. We conducted a within-subjects online 
experiment with 46 participants, comparing a baseline route-planning interface with an interactive web-application prototype First-Mile 
Last-Mile (FMLM) interface across realistic Bangkok travel scenarios. The prototype improved subjective ratings for decision ease 
(+0.396, p = .005) and FMLM understanding (+0.526, p < .001), and 29 of 46 participants rated FMLM information on the positive side 
of the usefulness scale. However, calculated preference-aligned route choice did not improve at the full-sample level (mean difference = -0.005, p = .672), suggesting a gap between perceived usefulness and measurable route-alignment outcomes. Qualitative feedback 
indicates that FMLM information is valuable, especially for unfamiliar routes, but current interface complexity, terminology, map 
encoding, and route-data trust limit its effectiveness. These findings highlight design requirements for FMLM-aware route-planning tools.
